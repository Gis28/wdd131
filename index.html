Morocco

Morocco has a long history dating back nearly 300,000 years, highlighted by early Homo sapiens fossils at Jebel Irhoud. Over time, the Amazigh peoples shaped the region, followed by Phoenician, Carthaginian, and Roman influences such as Volubilis. Islam arrived in the 8th century, leading to the founding of the first Moroccan state under the Idrisids. Great Amazigh dynasties like the Almoravids, Almohads, and Marinids later expanded their power across North Africa and Andalusia. After the Saadians and the rise of the Alaouite dynasty in the 17th century, Morocco became a French and Spanish protectorate in 1912 before gaining independence in 1956. Today, it is a constitutional monarchy with a diverse identity rooted in Arab, Amazigh, and African cultures.
